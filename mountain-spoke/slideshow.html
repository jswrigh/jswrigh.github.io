We Have the Bike of Your Dreams

Buying a mountain bike is like buying a car. Anything with pedals and tires can get you from one place to another. In fact, a basic, inexpensive bike works great for beginners. If you are a casual cyclist or don't have a big bike budget, we can get you set up with an inexpensive (NOT cheap) new bike or a better pre-owned one for a few hundred dollars. If you are about to compete in the world championship mountain race, we can assemble, tune, and test the winning cycle for many, many thousands of bucks. Here are some examples of bikes we usually have in stock. You really need to come to the shop, though, so we can get to know you and work together to find your baby. We can order just about any bike made anywhere in the world.

The Diamondback Hook is a great beginner's bike. It has eight gears and disk brakes. Take it home for $499.
$499 also buys the GT Karakorum. It is also a wonderful starter bike with 11 gears.
The R2000 by Tommaso is a great value. It uses high-performance components at an almost unbelievable $699 price. Shimano gears and other quality parts make this a favorite.
Move up to the Fuji Bighorn to get a frame made of A6 aluminum, 11 speeds, and internal cable routing. Very reasonably priced at $1999
